About Us

Punjab Daanish School Jand Attock, established in 2012, is part of the Punjab Daanish Schools and Centres of Excellence Authority, a visionary initiative by former Chief Minister Shehbaz Sharif. Located in Tehsil Jand, 100 km from Islamabad, our Boys’ campus spans 183 acres, and the Girls’ campus covers 42 acres. We provide free, high-quality education to underprivileged children, fostering academic excellence and character development.

Admissions

Admissions for Grade VI are open annually, with a merit-based process. Each campus admits 110 students, prioritizing those from low-income families (below PKR 15,000/month). The entrance test in December covers English, Math, Science, Urdu, and IQ. Apply by December 8, 2024, for the 2025 session.

Eligibility

Students aged 10-12 who have passed Grade V, with parental income below PKR 15,000/month for free seats.

Process

Submit forms by December 8, 2024, take the entrance test on December 26, 2024, and await merit lists.

Scholarships

Full scholarships for 99% of students, covering tuition, boarding, and expenses.

Academic Programs

Our BISE Lahore-registered curriculum spans Grade VI to F.Sc., with small classes (30-35 students) for personalized learning. Subjects include English, Mathematics, General Science, Urdu, and Social Studies, with Pre-Medical, Pre-Engineering, and Computer Science streams at higher levels.

Facilities

Our campuses feature modern classrooms, science and computer labs, libraries, and comfortable boarding facilities. Sports grounds, auditoriums, and recreational spaces ensure a balanced lifestyle for students.

Academic Facilities

Modern labs, libraries, and smart classrooms for enhanced learning.

Boarding

Safe, comfortable dormitories with 24/7 supervision.

Recreation

Sports fields, gyms, and cultural spaces for student development.

Faculty

Our 484-strong faculty across the Daanish network includes qualified teachers for core subjects. At Jand Attock, educators like Prof. Sahibzada Naeem Ur Rasool and Ms. Riffat Naz set high standards, fostering critical thinking and academic excellence.

Co-Curricular Activities

We promote holistic development through sports, arts, debates, and cultural events. Students engage in inter-school competitions, science fairs, and environmental initiatives like the “Plant for Pakistan” campaign, planting over 42,000 saplings.
